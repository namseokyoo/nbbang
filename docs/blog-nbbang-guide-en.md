## Step 1: Add Participants

Start by adding everyone who's part of the bill.

![Add participants screen](/projects/nbbang/screenshots/blog/step1-add-participants.png)

**How to do it:**
1. Type a name in the input field
2. Press Enter or click the add button
3. Repeat until everyone's added

> **Tip**: You can add up to 20 participants. Any language works for names.

For example, if Alex, Jamie, Sam, Taylor, and Jordan went out together, add each name one by one.

---

## Step 2: Enter Amounts by Round

Now enter the amount spent at each stop. The key feature of N-ppang is that you can **select different participants for each round**.

![Add round amounts screen](/projects/nbbang/screenshots/blog/step2-add-round.png)

**How to do it:**
1. In Round 1, enter the total amount (e.g., $120)
2. Select who paid (e.g., Alex)
3. Check everyone who participated (or select all)
4. Move to Round 2 and repeat the process

**Real Example:**
- **Round 1 (Dinner)**: $120, all 5 people joined, Alex paid
- **Round 2 (Bar)**: $80, only Alex/Jamie/Sam (3 people) joined, Jamie paid
- **Round 3 (Snacks)**: $40, Alex/Jamie/Taylor/Jordan (4 people) joined, Sam paid

Different people at different rounds? No problem. N-ppang automatically calculates what each person owes.

## Step 3: Check the Results - Who Owes Whom?

Once you've entered everything, the settlement results appear automatically.

![Settlement results screen](/projects/nbbang/screenshots/nbbang-result.png)

**What you'll see:**
- The actual amount each person should pay
- Who needs to send money to whom
- A summary of the total settlement

**What's the "Minimum Transfers" Algorithm?**

Let's say A owes B $10, and B owes C $10. Instead of two transfers, N-ppang optimizes this so **A sends $10 directly to C**. This reduces the number of transactions and makes settling up much simpler.

> **The Bottom Line**: Let N-ppang handle the complex math. You just check the results and send the money.
